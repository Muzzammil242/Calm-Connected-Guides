Evidence-based. Not woo.

Every part of this guide is grounded in peer-reviewed neuroscience.

UCLA · Psychological Science

Putting feelings into words — called "affect labeling" — is linked with reduced amygdala activity during emotional processing. Naming what you feel literally calms your brain's alarm system.

JAMA Internal Medicine

A review of 47 randomised clinical trials (3,515 participants) found mindfulness programs showed moderate evidence for improving anxiety, depression, and pain.

Frontiers in Human Neuroscience

Slow-breathing techniques are linked with changes in autonomic, psychological, and brain activity related to emotional control and well-being (2018).
